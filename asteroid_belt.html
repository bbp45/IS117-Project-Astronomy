Asteroids and Comets




Even though, many debris formed into planets over time, when solar system formed initially, there were still many debris that never formed into planets. There are different types of these debris. The types include asteroids, comets, meteoroids, and meteorite. Both asteroids and comets orbit the Sun, but asteroids are rocky objects, whereas comets are made of ice and dust. Many asteroids and comets are discovered every year. The ones that orbits within our inner solar system are called NEOs, also known as near-Earth objects. There also are the ones that usually pose a threat to our planet. On the other hand, a meteoroid or meteor is a small space rock, which often enters the atmosphere of the Earth and burns up as soon as it enters. It is considered a meteor shower, when multiple meteoroids are entering and burning up in the atmosphere of Earth. The word meteor itself comes from a Greek word, meteoron.
