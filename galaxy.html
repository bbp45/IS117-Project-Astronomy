Galaxy



There are many galaxies that exist in this universe. The galaxy where the solar system and our home planet, Earth, resides is the Milky Way galaxy. It consists of billions of stars. They are either single or double stars. There are also many star clusters within it. There are three types to these clusters : globular clusters, open clusters, and stellar associations. It is approximately 13.6 billion years old. The disk of the galaxy is about a hundred thousand light years in diameter, and one thousand light years thick. There is a supermassive black hole in the very center of the Milky Way, which can consume anything that gets closer to it. It is known as Sagittarius A* and it was discovered by Bruce Balick and Robert Brown. In fact, the first picture of it was released three years ago in 2019. However, it was initially discovered back in 1974. Furthermore, Milky Way is not the only galaxy in the universe. There are billions of galaxies too, and most of them are unknown to the mankind. There are some neighboring galaxies to our Milky Way galaxy, one of which is Andromeda galaxy. It is the nearest one and both Milky Way and Andromeda are directly headed toward each other. It is predicted that in about five billion years in the future, they will collide and eventually merge together. If humanity manages to survive even one billion years from now, they will be able to see the galaxy appear large enough for them to see it with their naked eyes. If they survive five billion years into the future, they will be to live in the new merged galaxy, maybe called Milkdromeda.
