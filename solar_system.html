Solar System



The solar system is a system that exists within our home galaxy, the Milky May. It is also where our home planet, Earth, resides. According to one of the theories by the scientists, the solar system was formed from a solar nebula a little less than 5 billion years ago, around the same time as the Earth's formation. Originally, there were nine planets as Pluto was counted as one of the planets until 2006. Afterwards, it got categorized as a dwarf planet. Now, there are eight planets in total: four rockey terrestrial planets and four gas giants. The four rockey terrestrial planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. They mainly are made of rock and are the closest to the sun. The four gas giants also known as Jovian planets are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. They mainly are made of either gases or ice and are farthest from the sun. Some of these planets apparently even have their own moons, such as our home planet, Earth, Jupiter, Saturn, etc. Earth only has one moon. However, Jupiter is known to have about eighty moons and Saturn is known to have over sixty moons.
